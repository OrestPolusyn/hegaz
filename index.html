Why liquid helium is worth buying from HEGAZ?

Liquid helium is supplied in vessels designed for cryogenic applications - Dewar flasks. The volume of special vessels is 250 liters.

Due to its low boiling point (-268.9oC) and its property of creating a magnetic field without additional heating, helium is widely used in medical diagnostics by magnetic resonance imaging (MRI).

All modern superconducting MRI scanners with magnetic field strengths of 1.5 and 3.0 Tesla use liquid helium. It is transported and stored in special vessels - Dewar vessels made of stainless steel or aluminum.

HEGAZ offers liquid helium for MRI.

Why do you need helium for an MRI scanner?

MRI machines use helium in the cooling system of scanners.

To ensure that the equipment functions correctly, the amount of liquid helium in the MRI cryostat needs to be monitored regularly to ensure that it does not go below 30%.

It is common to refill the cooling system with helium once every four years. Individual tomograph models designed with a zero helium evaporation system require refilling even less frequently, once every ten years.
